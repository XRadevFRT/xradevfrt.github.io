Major Summit Reaches Historic Climate Accord

World leaders agree on binding emissions targets; scientists call it a turning point.

Published 25 Feb 2026 Â· 4 min read

Leaders from more than 190 countries have agreed to a landmark climate deal that commits nations to binding emissions reductions by 2030. The accord, reached after two weeks of intense negotiations, has been hailed as a historic turning point by scientists and campaigners.

The agreement includes substantial funding for developing nations and a mechanism to review progress every five years. Several major economies announced new net-zero targets in the final hours of the summit.

Environmental groups welcomed the outcome but warned that implementation will be the real test. Markets reacted positively to the news, with clean-energy stocks rising sharply in early trading.
